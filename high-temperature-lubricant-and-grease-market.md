<p><p>The high-temperature lubricant and grease market is categorized primarily into two types: soap thickeners and non-soap thickeners. Soap thickeners, often made from lithium, calcium, or sodium soaps, provide excellent mechanical stability and water resistance, making them suitable for high-temperature applications. Non-soap thickeners, including synthetic products like polyurea and clay-based formulations, offer enhanced thermal stability and performance in extreme conditions. Both categories cater to industries requiring reliable lubrication in high-heat environments, ensuring optimal equipment efficiency and longevity.</p></p>
